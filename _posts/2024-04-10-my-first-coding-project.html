The week's question on the Codedex learning platform was: “What is your favourite place to code?”

I did not think long and just started writing. After diving into my memories, this text came out.

My response to this question might evoke some nostalgia and amusement… Please refrain from laughing too loudly because I might join in!

Let me take you back to when I embarked on my first coding adventure: creating a website dedicated to the legendary Diablo PC game. Picture this — it was 1996, and the internet was the Next Big Thing. Armed with a bright yellow book titled “HTML for Dummies” that I stumbled upon in the library, I dove headfirst into the world of web development.

HTML was refreshingly simple back then, and armed with just a dash of JavaScript 1.1 knowledge, I set out to bring my Diablo tribute to life. My trusty companion? None other than notepad.exe — the unsung hero of my coding journey.

Despite having limited computer access — a mere 1 hour and 30 minutes per week — I was undeterred. My favourite coding spot? The bus, where I meticulously crafted HTML pages in thick notebooks. Once a week, I would eagerly type out my handwritten code, bringing to fruition what would become the “Site de référence par excellence sur le jeu Diablo” — a modest success that garnered attention in magazines and fueled my burgeoning love for coding and creating.

Fast forward 25+ years, and here I am, reminiscing like an old record…

Ah, but wait! There’s more. For those curious souls, I recently unearthed an old backup of the website, now preserved for posterity on my GitHub. Behold, my Diablo time capsule:

My Diablo Website — 1996

https://github.com/TheRealFREDP3D/My-Diablo-Website-1996/blob/main/index.html

Take a stroll down memory lane and witness firsthand the humble beginnings of web development. It’s a testament to the journey that sparked my enduring passion for coding and learning.

So, what started as a simple coding project all those years ago has blossomed into a lifelong journey filled with discovery and wonder. Who knew that a fascination with a PC game would pave the way for a love affair with technology?
